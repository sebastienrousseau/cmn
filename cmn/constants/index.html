The constants module contains the Constants structure, which provides a collection of constant values that are used throughout the library.

Constants

  • AVOGADRO
    Avogadro’s constant Approximately 6.02214076 x 10^23
  • BOLTZMANN
    Boltzmann’s constant Approximately 1.380648 x 10^-23
  • EULER
    The base of the natural logarithm, Euler’s number (e). e ≈ 2.7182818284590452353602874713527
  • GAMMA
    The mathematical constant γ or the Euler–Mascheroni constant. It is the limit of the difference between the harmonic series and the natural logarithm of the natural numbers.
  • HASH_ALGORITHM
    The hash algorithm used. The default is Blake3.
  • HASH_COST
    The cost of the hash algorithm. The default is 8.
  • HASH_LENGTH
    The hash length is the length of the hash in bytes.
  • PHI
    The mathematical constant φ or the golden ratio. It is the limit of the ratio of consecutive Fibonacci numbers. Φ = (1+√5)/2 = 2.cos(π/5). Diagonal of a unit-side pentagon.
  • PI
    The mathematical constant π or the ratio of a circle’s circumference to its diameter.
  • PLANCK
    The Planck constant, h.
  • SILVER_RATIO
    The mathematical constant `δs’ or the silver ratio (or silver mean). δs = 1+√2. One of the silver means (n+sqrt(n2+1))/2 for n>0.
  • SPECIAL_CHARS
    A set of special characters.
  • SQRT2
    The mathematical constant √2 or the Pythagora’s constant or the square root of 2. It is the diagonal of a square with unit side length. 2 = 1 + √2
  • SQRT3
    The mathematical constant √3 or the principal square root of 3. It is the length of the side of an equilateral triangle with unit side length. 3 = 1 + √3
  • SQRT5
    The mathematical constant √5 or the principal square root of 5. It is the length of the diagonal of a regular pentagon with unit side length. 5 = 2 + 2√5
  • TAU
    The mathematical constant τ or the ratio of a circle’s circumference to its radius.
